Q1 Ch. 2 — Descriptive Statistics
A wildlife researcher records the weights (in kg) of 15 grizzly bears:

3.2, 4.1, 4.5, 4.8, 5.0, 5.2, 5.5, 5.7, 6.0, 6.3, 6.5, 6.8, 7.2, 8.1, 22.4

Which of the following best describes the appropriate measures of center and spread for this data?
  • A Mean and standard deviation, because the data is quantitative.
  • B Median and IQR, because the outlier 22.4 makes the data skewed.
  • C Mean and IQR, because IQR is always preferred for spread.
  • D Median and standard deviation, because the median is resistant.
  • E Mean and median together, as both are always needed.
Answer: B — When data has outliers or is skewed, the median and IQR are preferred because they are resistant to outliers. The value 22.4 is a suspected outlier (check: Q₃ + 1.5·IQR), which would strongly pull the mean and inflate the standard deviation.
Q2Ch. 3 — Correlation
A researcher computes the correlation between height (in inches) and weight (in pounds) for a sample of adults and gets r = 0.72. If the researcher converts all heights to centimeters and all weights to kilograms, what will the new correlation be?
  • A It will decrease, because the units are now smaller.
  • B It will increase, because metric units are more precise.
  • C r = 0.72, because correlation does not change when units are scaled.
  • D r = −0.72, because the direction reverses with metric units.
  • E Cannot be determined without the original data.
Answer: C — Correlation r does not change when you change units (scale the data) or swap the x and y variables. It is a unitless measure of linear association.
Q3Ch. 4 — Regression
A regression line for predicting a student's exam score (y) from hours studied (x) is ŷ = 42 + 5.8x. A student studies for 7 hours and scores 83. What is the residual for this student?
  • A −0.6
  • B +0.6
  • C −82.6
  • D +82.6
  • E 0
Answer: B — Residual = y − ŷ. First compute ŷ = 42 + 5.8(7) = 42 + 40.6 = 82.6. Then residual = 83 − 82.6 = +0.6. The point is above the regression line (positive residual).
Q4Ch. 9–10 — Probability
A bag contains 4 red and 6 blue marbles. Two marbles are drawn without replacement. What is the probability that both marbles drawn are red?
  • A 0.16
  • B 0.133
  • C 0.40
  • D 0.267
  • E 0.24
Answer: B — Without replacement: P(both red) = P(1st red) × P(2nd red | 1st red) = (4/10) × (3/9) = 12/90 = 2/15 ≈ 0.133. Use the General Multiplication Rule since events are not independent.
Q5Ch. 11 — Normal Distribution
The daily rainfall in a rainforest is Normally distributed with μ = 18 mm and σ = 4 mm. What is the probability that on a randomly selected day, rainfall exceeds 24 mm?
  • A 0.9332
  • B 0.1587
  • C 0.0668
  • D 0.8413
  • E 0.3085
Answer: C — z = (24 − 18)/4 = 1.5. P(X > 24) = P(Z > 1.5) = 1 − P(Z ≤ 1.5) = 1 − 0.9332 = 0.0668.
Q6Ch. 12 — Binomial Distribution
A factory produces bolts, and 8% of all bolts are defective. A quality inspector randomly selects 15 bolts. What are the mean and standard deviation of the number of defective bolts?
  • A μ = 1.2, σ ≈ 1.05
  • B μ = 1.2, σ ≈ 0.11
  • C μ = 0.08, σ ≈ 1.05
  • D μ = 1.38, σ ≈ 1.05
  • E μ = 1.2, σ ≈ 0.074
Answer: A — For B(n=15, p=0.08): μ = np = 15(0.08) = 1.2. σ = √(np(1−p)) = √(15 × 0.08 × 0.92) = √(1.104) ≈ 1.05.
Q7Ch. 13 — Sampling Distributions
The weights of adult male dogs of a certain breed are Normally distributed with μ = 28 kg and σ = 5 kg. If a random sample of 25 dogs is selected, what is the standard deviation of the sampling distribution of x̄?
  • A 5 kg
  • B 0.2 kg
  • C 1.0 kg
  • D 1.12 kg
  • E 25 kg
Answer: C — σ/√n = 5/√25 = 5/5 = 1.0 kg. This is also called the Standard Error (SE).
Q8Ch. 14 — Confidence Intervals
A botanist takes a random sample of 36 plants and finds the sample mean leaf length is 12.4 cm with σ = 3.0 cm (known). Which of the following is the correct 90% confidence interval for the population mean leaf length?
  • A (11.58, 13.22)
  • B (11.42, 13.38)
  • C (11.18, 13.62)
  • D (10.82, 13.98)
  • E (11.65, 13.15)
Answer: A — σ known → use z-interval. SE = 3/√36 = 0.5. z* = 1.645 for 90% CI. m = 1.645 × 0.5 = 0.8225. CI: 12.4 ± 0.8225 = (11.58, 13.22).
Q9Ch. 14–15 — Hypothesis Testing
A pharmaceutical company claims its new drug reduces LDL cholesterol by a mean of μ = 30 points. A researcher is skeptical and believes the reduction is less than 30 points. She collects a sample of 50 patients and finds x̄ = 27.5 with σ = 10 (known). What is the P-value?
  • A 0.0384
  • B 0.0768
  • C 0.9616
  • D 0.0192
  • E 0.4616
Answer: A — H₀: μ = 30, Hₐ: μ < 30 (one-sided). z = (27.5 − 30)/(10/√50) = −2.5/1.414 = −1.77. P-value = P(Z ≤ −1.77) ≈ 0.0384. Since Hₐ: μ < 30, the P-value is the left-tail area.
Q10Ch. 15 — Type I & II Errors
A medical researcher tests whether a new vaccine is effective. She sets α = 0.01 (very stringent). Compared to using α = 0.05, this choice:
  • A Decreases the probability of a Type I error and increases the probability of a Type II error.
  • B Increases the probability of both Type I and Type II errors.
  • C Increases the probability of a Type I error and decreases the probability of a Type II error.
  • D Has no effect on error probabilities; only sample size matters.
  • E Decreases the probability of both errors.
Answer: A — Lowering α reduces P(Type I error) = α, but this makes it harder to reject H₀, which increases P(Type II error) = β and decreases Power = 1 − β. There is always a tradeoff between Type I and Type II errors for fixed n.
Q11Ch. 17 — One-Sample t-test
A nutritionist claims the mean daily sugar intake of teenagers is 95 grams. A random sample of 20 teenagers has x̄ = 102 g and s = 18 g. The t-statistic for testing H₀: μ = 95 vs. Hₐ: μ ≠ 95 is:
  • A t = 1.74
  • B t = 1.38
  • C t = 0.39
  • D t = 7.78
  • E t = 2.12
Answer: A — t = (x̄ − μ₀)/(s/√n) = (102 − 95)/(18/√20) = 7/(18/4.472) = 7/4.025 ≈ 1.74, df = n−1 = 19.
Q12Ch. 17 — t Robustness
A researcher has a sample of n = 12 measurements that appear to be strongly skewed with two outliers. Which statement about using a one-sample t-procedure is correct?
  • A The t-procedure is valid because n ≥ 10.
  • B The t-procedure should NOT be used — with n < 15, data must be close to Normal with no outliers.
  • C The t-procedure is valid because t-distributions are robust to all violations.
  • D The t-procedure is valid because σ is unknown.
  • E The t-procedure is valid if we use df = n.
Answer: B — Robustness guidelines: n < 15 requires data to be close to Normal with NO outliers. With strong skew and two outliers at n = 12, the t-procedure is not appropriate. Consider a transformation or nonparametric method.
Q13Ch. 18 — Two-Sample t
Researchers compare the mean running speeds (m/s) of two species of lizards using independent random samples: Species A (n₁ = 12, x̄₁ = 3.8, s₁ = 0.6) and Species B (n₂ = 10, x̄₂ = 3.2, s₂ = 0.8). The SE for the difference x̄₁ − x̄₂ is approximately:
  • A 0.297
  • B 0.0882
  • C 0.60
  • D 0.140
  • E 0.529
Answer: A — SE = √(s₁²/n₁ + s₂²/n₂) = √(0.36/12 + 0.64/10) = √(0.030 + 0.064) = √(0.094) ≈ 0.297.
Q14Ch. 19 — Inference for Proportions
In a survey of 400 randomly selected Notre Dame students, 132 said they exercise at least 5 days per week. Which of the following is the correct 95% large-sample confidence interval for the population proportion p?
  • A (0.285, 0.375)
  • B (0.291, 0.369)
  • C (0.274, 0.386)
  • D (0.296, 0.364)
  • E (0.263, 0.397)
Answer: A — p̂ = 132/400 = 0.33. SE = √(0.33×0.67/400) ≈ 0.02350. m = 1.96 × 0.02350 = 0.0461. CI: 0.33 ± 0.046 ≈ (0.284, 0.376). Closest to option A.
Q15Ch. 19 — Sample Size
A pollster wants to estimate the proportion of voters who support a ballot measure with a margin of error of no more than 3% at the 95% confidence level. Using the most conservative estimate for p, what is the minimum required sample size?
  • A 752
  • B 1068
  • C 1537
  • D 900
  • E 384
Answer: B — Use p* = 0.5 (most conservative). n = (z*/m)² × p*(1−p*) = (1.96/0.03)² × 0.25 = (65.33)² × 0.25 ≈ 1067.1 → round up to 1068.
Q16Ch. 20 — Two Proportions
A clinical trial tests a new blood pressure medication. In the treatment group (n₁ = 200), 40 patients experienced side effects (p̂₁ = 0.20). In the control group (n₂ = 200), 60 patients experienced side effects (p̂₂ = 0.30). What is the Absolute Risk Reduction (ARR)?
  • A ARR = 0.10
  • B ARR = 0.33
  • C ARR = 0.50
  • D ARR = 0.20
  • E ARR = 3.0
Answer: A — ARR = p̂_control − p̂_treatment = 0.30 − 0.20 = 0.10. NNT = 1/ARR = 10. RRR = 0.10/0.30 ≈ 0.33.
Q17Ch. 21 — Goodness of Fit
An ecologist hypothesizes that four species of birds visit a feeder in equal proportions. She observes 200 total visits: Species A = 58, B = 46, C = 52, D = 44. What are the expected count for each species and the degrees of freedom for the chi-square test?
  • A Expected = 50 for each; df = 4
  • B Expected = 50 for each; df = 3
  • C Expected = 40 for each; df = 3
  • D Expected = 50 for each; df = 199
  • E Expected varies by species; df = 3
Answer: B — Uniform hypothesis: expected = n/k = 200/4 = 50 for each. df = k − 1 = 4 − 1 = 3.

Q19Ch. 22 — Chi-Square Hypotheses
For the chi-square test of independence in a two-way table, the null hypothesis states:
  • A The row variable causes the column variable.
  • B All expected counts are equal.
  • C There is no association between the row and column variables.
  • D The proportions in each row are equal to 1/c.
  • E Observed counts equal expected counts exactly.
Answer: C — H₀: There is no association between the row and column variables. Hₐ: There IS an association. Note that A is wrong — chi-square tests association, not causation.
Q20Ch. 24 — One-Way ANOVA
A researcher compares the mean reaction times (ms) of participants across three caffeine conditions (none, low, high) using ANOVA. The ANOVA table shows MSG = 850 and MSE = 210. What is the F-statistic, and what are the numerator and denominator degrees of freedom if there are k = 3 groups and N = 45 total observations?
  • A F = 4.05; df = (2, 42)
  • B F = 4.05; df = (3, 44)
  • C F = 0.247; df = (2, 42)
  • D F = 4.05; df = (2, 45)
  • E F = 4.29; df = (2, 42)
Answer: A — F = MSG/MSE = 850/210 ≈ 4.05. Numerator df = k − 1 = 2. Denominator df = N − k = 45 − 3 = 42. So df = (2, 42).
Q21Ch. 24 — ANOVA Assumptions
In a one-way ANOVA comparing four groups, the sample standard deviations are s₁ = 4.2, s₂ = 3.8, s₃ = 7.9, s₄ = 4.5. Is the equal variance assumption satisfied?
  • A Yes, because all standard deviations are less than 10.
  • B No — the largest s (7.9) is more than 2× the smallest s (3.8).
  • C Yes, because the largest variance (62.4) is less than 4× the smallest variance (14.4).
  • D Cannot be checked without the raw data.
  • E Yes, because the sample sizes are all equal.
Answer: B — Rule of thumb: largest sᵢ should be no more than ~2× smallest sᵢ. Here 7.9/3.8 ≈ 2.08 > 2 and the variance ratio is 4.32 > 4. Equal variance assumption is NOT satisfied.
Q22Ch. 26 — Two-Way ANOVA
In a two-way ANOVA with Factor R (3 levels) and Factor C (2 levels), what are the degrees of freedom for the interaction term?
  • A 5
  • B 6
  • C 2
  • D 1
  • E 3
Answer: C — Interaction df = (r−1)(c−1) = (3−1)(2−1) = 2 × 1 = 2. Main effect df: Factor R = 2; Factor C = 1.

Q25Ch. 21 — Chi-Square Goodness of Fit
A genetics researcher claims that a particular trait follows a 9:3:3:1 Mendelian ratio across four phenotypes (A, B, C, D). In an experiment with 160 offspring, the observed counts are:

PhenotypeABCDTotal
Observed92282614160

Test whether the observed data are consistent with the 9:3:3:1 ratio at α = 0.05.
Part A — State the hypotheses.
Write H₀ and Hₐ here…
Solution
H₀: The phenotype proportions follow a 9:3:3:1 ratio (p_A = 9/16, p_B = 3/16, p_C = 3/16, p_D = 1/16).
Hₐ: At least one of the proportions differs from the hypothesized value (the data do not follow a 9:3:3:1 ratio).
Part B — Calculate the expected counts. Check conditions.
Calculate expected counts and verify conditions…
Solution
Expected counts = n × pᵢ₀:
E_A = 160 × (9/16) = 90  E_B = 160 × (3/16) = 30  E_C = 160 × (3/16) = 30  E_D = 160 × (1/16) = 10

Conditions: (1) SRS ✓; (2) All expected counts ≥ 1: min = 10 ✓; (3) No more than 20% of expected counts < 5: 0 out of 4 are < 5 ✓. All conditions met.
Part C — Calculate the χ² statistic and find the P-value. State your conclusion.
Calculate χ², find df and P-value, write conclusion…
Solution
χ² = (92−90)²/90 + (28−30)²/30 + (26−30)²/30 + (14−10)²/10
= 4/90 + 4/30 + 16/30 + 16/10 = 0.044 + 0.133 + 0.533 + 1.600 = 2.311

df = k − 1 = 3. Using Table D: P-value > 0.25 (well above α = 0.05).

Conclusion: Since P-value > 0.05, we fail to reject H₀. The data are consistent with the 9:3:3:1 Mendelian ratio.
Q26Ch. 24 — One-Way ANOVA
A food scientist tests whether mean moisture content (%) differs among three types of crackers. Random samples are taken and summarized below:

Cracker Typenx̄s
Whole Wheat812.41.8
Sourdough814.12.2
Rye813.01.6

Overall mean x̄ = 13.17. Test at α = 0.05.
Part A — State the hypotheses and check the equal variance assumption.
Write hypotheses and check conditions…
Solution
H₀: μ₁ = μ₂ = μ₃   Hₐ: Not all means are equal.

Equal variance check: Largest s = 2.2, Smallest s = 1.6. Ratio = 2.2/1.6 = 1.375 < 2 ✓. Assumption is satisfied.
Part B — Calculate MSG and MSE.
Show your calculations for MSG and MSE…
Solution
MSG = Σnᵢ(x̄ᵢ − x̄)² / (k−1) = [8(0.5929) + 8(0.8649) + 8(0.0289)] / 2 = 11.893/2 = 5.947

MSE = Σ(nᵢ−1)sᵢ² / (N−k) = [7(3.24) + 7(4.84) + 7(2.56)] / 21 = 74.48/21 = 3.547
Part C — Calculate F, find the P-value, and state a conclusion.
Calculate F-statistic and write conclusion…
Solution
F = MSG/MSE = 5.947/3.547 = 1.677, df = (2, 21).

P-value > 0.10 (F is well below F₀.₁₀ ≈ 2.57 for df 2,21).

Conclusion: Since P-value > 0.05, we fail to reject H₀. There is not sufficient evidence that mean moisture content differs among the three types of crackers.
Q27Ch. 17–18 — Matched Pairs & Interpretation
A physical therapist measures patients' pain scores (0–10) before and after a new treatment. Data for 7 patients:

Patient1234567
Before7869578
After4556346
Difference (Before−After)3313232

The mean difference is x̄_diff = 2.43 and s_diff = 0.787.
Part A — Why is a matched pairs design appropriate here? State the hypotheses.
Explain and state hypotheses…
Solution
Why matched pairs: The same 7 patients are measured twice. The two measurements are paired — not independent samples. This controls for individual differences in baseline pain levels.

H₀: μ_diff = 0 (the treatment has no effect)
Hₐ: μ_diff > 0 (the treatment reduces pain, i.e., Before − After > 0 on average)
Part B — Carry out the t-test at α = 0.05. Calculate t, find the P-value range, and state a conclusion.
Show all work for the hypothesis test…
Solution
t = (x̄_diff − 0) / (s_diff/√n) = 2.43 / (0.787/√7) = 2.43 / 0.2974 = 8.17, df = 6.

From Table C with df = 6, one-sided: t = 8.17 >> t₀.₀₀₀₅ = 5.959 → P-value < 0.0005.

Conclusion: Since P-value < 0.05, we reject H₀. The data provide very strong evidence that the treatment significantly reduces pain scores (mean reduction ≈ 2.43 points).
